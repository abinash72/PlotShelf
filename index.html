Inside Out
One fine day, a young elephant was bathing in the river and playing around while waiting for her crush: a tall handsome giraffe. And as she anticipated, he did come strolling to have a sip of water. The elephant sighed and murmured, "Perfect timing." Just a little glance was enough to make her blush. The giraffe gave her a look, she started acting as if no one was around. The giraffe said, "Hey, how is there water in this river, after you are inside it?" and started laughing out loud. With a heavy lump in her throat, the elephant ran away. “What happened?" The elephant’s mother asked as she reached home weeping. After she heard the story with a heavy heart, she said, “Your shape doesn’t define your identity, what’s inside matters.”

Happiness
“Dad, why do we need ads? I just want to see cricket.” “Beta, they are there so we can choose.” “What if I am just satisfied with what I have?” “Oh, you are a danger to society. Nobody will like you. You don’t want that, do you?” “Not at all, dad,” the child said. The next day, he turned in his report on ads in school. The teacher saw it and asked him, “What do you mean you like ads because you want people to like you?” “I mean if I don’t like the things people like, how will they like me? The Foolish Ass
One day as an Ass was walking in the pasture, he found some Grasshoppers chirping merrily in a grassy corner of the field. He listened with a great deal of admiration to the song of the Grasshoppers. It was such a joyful song that his pleasure-loving heart was filled with a wish to sing as they did. "What is it?" he asked very respectfully, "that has given you such beautiful voices? Is there any special food you eat or is it some divine nectar that makes you sing so wonderfully?" "Yes," said the Grasshoppers, who were very fond of jokes, "it is the dew we drink! Try some and see." So thereafter the Ass would eat nothing and drink nothing but dew. Naturally, the poor foolish Ass died.
